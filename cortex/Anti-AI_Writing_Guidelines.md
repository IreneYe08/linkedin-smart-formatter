# ANTI-AI WRITING GUIDELINES
*How to avoid the "AI Vibe" and maintain a human voice.*

---

## 1. THE "RED FLAG" PATTERNS (SPECIFIC)

### **The Em Dash (—) Obsession**
- **Pattern:** Frequent use of the long em dash to separate thoughts.
- **Human Fix:** Use commas, periods, or colons instead. Don't let every post have 2-3 em dashes.

### **Parallel Sentence Structures**
- **Pattern:** "It's not just about [X], it's about [Y]."
- **Human Fix:** Vary your sentence starters. Avoid this specific "not just X" logic which AI uses as a default transition.

### **The Rule of Three (Lists)**
- **Pattern:** Grouping adjectives or benefits in groups of three (e.g., "fast, reliable, and efficient").
- **Human Fix:** Use two points, or four. Break the predictable rhythm of three.

### **Generic Transitions (The "Connector" Tell)**
- **Pattern:** Using "safe" transitions like "That said," "Here's the thing," or "Let's break this down."
- **Human Fix:** Jump directly to the next point. Use a specific observation to bridge thoughts instead of a canned phrase.

### **Generic / Over-the-Top Praise**
- **Pattern:** Using "glazing" language like "captivating," "vivid storytelling," or "imaginative genius."
- **Human Fix:** Be specific. Instead of "I love your storytelling," say "I liked that specific story about the eels."

---

## 2. VOCABULARY TO AVOID (THE "DELVE" LIST)
AI leans on "safe" but robotic vocabulary. Avoid or minimize these:
- **Verbs:** Delve, Elevate, Foster, Unleash, Transform, Optimize.
- **Adjectives:** Innovative, Practical, Comprehensive, Seamless, Vibrant, Captivating.
- **Phrases:** "In today's fast-paced world," "Look no further," "Testament to."

---

## 3. THE STRUCTURAL TELLS

### **The "Empty Meaning" Preamble**
- **The Tell:** Starting with a general fact that everyone knows (e.g., "Social media is changing how we connect...").
- **Human Fix:** Get straight to the point or start with a personal anecdote.

### **Over-Explanation & Restating**
- **The Tell:** Restating the same point in slightly different words to "clarify" it.
- **Human Fix:** Say it once, say it well, and move on.

### **Weird Analogies**
- **The Tell:** Using analogies that technically make sense but feel forced or "try-hard" (e.g., "like a lighthouse in a dense fog").

---

## 4. HOW TO PROVE YOU ARE HUMAN (THE "VIBE CHECK")
- **Ground Your Claims:** No abstraction without grounding. Every claim needs a concrete reference, a number, or a specific scenario (e.g., instead of "It saves time," say "It saved me 3 hours of copy-pasting on Tuesday").
- **Inject Personality:** Use "I" and "me." Share specific personal stories or locations (e.g., "When I was in London last week...").
- **Go on Tangents:** Robots follow a straight line (A to B). Humans make relevant but slightly off-topic connections.
- **Pass the Vibe Check:** Trust your gut. If it feels "corporately kind" or insincere, it's likely AI.
- **First-Person Action:** Use "I have to take my hat off to you" instead of the passive "Hats off to you."
